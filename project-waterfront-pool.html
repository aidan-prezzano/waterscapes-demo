Story

The pool disappears into the Gulf. That was the only brief.

The clients owned a waterfront lot in Orange Beach with an unobstructed view to the Gulf. They had one instruction: they wanted to sit in the spa and feel like the pool and the water beyond were the same thing. Everything else was detail.

We set the vanishing edge on the Gulf-facing side, with the overflow trough hidden below the sight line from both the house and the spa. The pool runs eighteen meters along the view axis. The dark charcoal pebble finish makes the water read nearly black in the afternoon — absorbing light rather than reflecting it, so the Gulf's silver surface beyond reads in sharp contrast.

The spa sits at the opposite end, elevated above the pool deck, with a single spillover return. The water sound is deliberate — continuous, low, and not competing with the Gulf. On still days, the Gulf wins. On windy days, they're equal.

"Standing in the spa, you genuinely cannot tell where our pool ends and the Gulf begins. That's exactly what we wanted." Kevin & Melissa W. — Clients

The site presented a challenge: Orange Beach soil at that elevation is almost pure sand. The shell required additional rebar density and a thicker gunite mix — a specification we've refined across a dozen coastal Alabama builds. The equipment vault was poured into a concrete cradle to prevent settling over time.

Twelve months from first site visit to final swim. The view is unchanged.
