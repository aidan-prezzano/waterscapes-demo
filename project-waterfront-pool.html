Story

Four kids, one brief: a pool big enough to actually swim in.

The family had four kids and a Spanish Fort backyard that had been a flat lawn for ten years. The brief was not complicated: they wanted a real pool, a diving board, and enough concrete deck to set chairs around. No resort. No features. Just a pool their kids could use every day.

We built a fourteen-by-seven-meter rectangle in a bright blue vinyl liner with a concrete deck all the way around. The deep end drops to 2.4 meters to accommodate the diving board, which is anchored into a poured concrete insert — not surface-mounted hardware. The aluminum safety fence was added at their request and runs the full perimeter, gated at two points.

This is a pool that does its job without asking for attention. The liner is a straightforward bright blue — the family picked it in the first ten minutes, never looked back. The concrete is brushed and sloped away from the pool edge. The equipment pad is tucked at the back of the lot behind the fence line.

"The kids were in it the day it filled. We've had more family time this summer than in the last five years combined." Kevin & Lisa B. — Clients

What matters on a build like this is what you don't see: the wall engineering rated for this soil type, the floor contour pitched correctly toward the main drain, the coping seated flush so no one catches a toe on an edge. The liner on a pool like this will need replacing at year ten or twelve. The shell underneath will outlast the house.

Five months from permit to first cannonball. The neighborhood kids know where to be on a Saturday.
