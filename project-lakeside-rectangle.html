Story

A clean line of water set against a Baldwin County sky.

The clients wanted a lap pool that felt like it belonged to the landscape — not a rectangle dropped into a lawn. The lot backed onto a retention pond with a long sight line to the east. We oriented the pool to that view.

The pool is fifteen meters long and runs parallel to the pond edge, separated by a low stone retaining wall that doubles as an informal bench. From inside the water, the far coping disappears against the sky. The effect was intentional — the same principle as a vanishing edge, executed with a hard material edge rather than a water blade.

The integrated spa sits at the near end, elevated eight inches above pool deck, with a spillover return that creates a constant soft sound of moving water. The white plaster finish keeps the color bright even in the long afternoon shade of the pine stand at the western lot line.

"We wanted something simple that we wouldn't get tired of. Fifteen years from now, this pool will look exactly right." Tom & Angela M. — Clients

The travertine coping was sourced from a single lot so the vein pattern reads continuously around the perimeter — a detail most clients never notice consciously but always feel. The LED light package was specified to operate at a fixed warm white with no color-change mode. One decision, correctly made.

Ten months from permit to final inspection. The pond beyond has been undisturbed.
