Story

A well-built liner pool for a family that wanted to swim, not impress.

The clients had a Midtown Mobile backyard, a clear budget, and a clear brief: a clean rectangle they could swim in by June. No spa, no waterfalls, nothing complicated. Just a pool, done right.

We built a vinyl liner pool in a blue speckle finish with a decorative tile border at the waterline. The coping is standard brushed concrete — durable, maintenance-easy, and honest about what it is. The shell was excavated and poured on schedule. The liner went in mid-week. They were swimming by that Friday.

Liner pools have a reputation as the entry-level option, and in one sense they are — but a well-built liner pool lasts twenty years. The liner itself gets changed at ten or twelve. What matters is the prep work underneath: the wall thickness, the floor contour, the way the coping is seated. We don't cut corners on any of it regardless of what the surface finish is.

"We weren't looking for the fanciest pool on the block. We wanted one that was built right and would last. That's exactly what we got." Marcus & Denise R. — Clients

This is bread-and-butter Waterscapes work — the kind of project we built our name on before "custom pool builder" became a marketing term. A tight excavation, a clean shell, a quality liner, and a family that can actually use their backyard. Four months from permit to first swim.

The tile border at the waterline is a decorative touch the clients chose from our sample book. They picked it in the first ten minutes. Some decisions are easy.
