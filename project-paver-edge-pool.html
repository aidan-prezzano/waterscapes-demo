Story

Designed around a sixty-year-old oak tree that nobody wanted to lose.

The clients had a Midtown Mobile backyard with a mature live oak at its center. Every contractor they spoke to told them the tree would have to go. We disagreed — and designed the pool around it instead.

The freeform shape follows the oak's canopy drip line. The paver deck wraps the trunk at a safe distance and steps down to the water's edge in a broad curved section that reads as a natural beach entry. The pool is deeper on the shaded side, shallower on the sun side, creating two distinct zones for swimming and lounging without a visible divider.

The finish is pebble-tec in a dark slate tone — not the typical turquoise. In the dappled light under the oak, the water shifts from charcoal at noon to silver at dusk. The travertine pavers were laid in a herringbone pattern around the curved edge and transitioned to a straight running bond at the property line.

"Every other contractor told us the tree had to come down. Clay designed the whole pool around it. That told us everything we needed to know." Robert & Diane F. — Clients

Construction required hand-digging within six feet of the root system — no mechanical excavators. The shell was poured in two sections to allow the root zone to be hand-backfilled with aerated soil mix. The equipment pad sits at the far corner of the lot, fully screened by the existing fence line.

Eight months from permit to first swim. The oak is still standing.
