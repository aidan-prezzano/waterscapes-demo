Story

A five-star resort experience in a Spanish Fort backyard.

The clients had a large, flat lot in a Spanish Fort subdivision and a clear vision: they wanted a backyard their family would never want to leave. A rock waterfall. Fire bowls. A swim-up shelf. The kind of pool you see in the magazines and assume is only for hotels.

We designed a sixteen-by-eight-meter freeform pool with a grotto beneath a four-tiered natural rock waterfall. The waterfall uses recirculated pool water — no separate pump system — and can be adjusted from a trickle to a full cascade via the automation panel inside the house. The two fire bowls flank the spa at the far end, plumbed for propane with in-pool ignition so neither requires a separate lighter.

The finish is pebble-tec in a blue lagoon blend — the color shifts from turquoise at the shallow sun shelf to deep blue-green in the grotto. At night, the color-changing LED system can run the full spectrum, but the default we set for the family is a steady teal.

"Our kids have friends over every weekend. We basically built the neighborhood pool. Couldn't be happier." David & Christine B. — Clients

The outdoor kitchen surround was added in the second phase — a poured concrete counter with a drop-in grill, two burners, and a fridge — set on the same travertine paver deck that wraps the pool. The entire backyard reads as one continuous designed space rather than a pool with furniture added around it.

Eleven months from permit to first splash. The neighborhood has not been the same since.
