Story

The pool was already there. The waterfall is what made it theirs.

The clients had an existing backyard pool — well-built, ten years old, in good shape. They were happy with it. But they kept looking at one corner of the yard and feeling like something was unfinished. They called us about a waterfall.

We built a semicircular raised wall in natural stone block at the pool's near end, with two stainless waterfall blades set into the face at water level. The blades are adjustable from the equipment pad — from a thin quiet curtain to a full cascade — so the family can dial in what they want on a given evening. The stone is dry-stacked in warm grey block that picks up the existing coping color without trying to match it exactly.

The LED lighting is integrated under the blade housings. At dusk, the falling water catches the light and the rest of the yard goes quiet around it. This is what a well-placed water feature does — it gives the eye somewhere to go. The pool is the same pool it always was. The backyard is a completely different place.

"We didn't think we needed a waterfall. Now we can't imagine the yard without it. It changed the whole feel of the space." Daniel & Renee H. — Clients

The structural work took two weeks — footing, wall, plumbing tie-in to the existing recirculation system. No new pump required; the waterfall runs off the existing variable-speed unit, which was already oversized for the pool. The remaining four weeks were finish work: stone facing, coping cap, blade installation, lighting, and commissioning.

Six weeks total. The clients were back in the pool the same afternoon we finished.
